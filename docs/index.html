Self-Driven Particles
Self-Driven Particles (SDP) describe one common type of multi-agent systems in daily life, such as fish school, flocking birds and traffic flows. However, it is expensive and time-consuming to simulate SDP through rules and heuristics, which motivates us to seek an automatic way to achieve this. Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) is appealing to lean controllers and then actuate the group automatically. Existing methods fall into three traditional MARL categories: fully-cooperative, fully-competitive and mixed both, which fail to capture the essence of SDP: each agent pursues its own goal and constantly changes its cooperative or competitive behaviors with its nearby agents. Behaviors of CoPO Agents
To simulate one of the common multi-agent systems, Self-Driven Particles(SDP), we propose coordinated policy optimization (CoPO). Exploiting local coordination and global coordination, the behaviors of CoPO agents reflect the key features of SDP: the relationship between agents are varied and dynamic over time. ADD VIDEO

Experiment Result
Supported by MetaDrive Simulator, we simulate the SDP in five typical traffic scenarios: roundabout, intersection, bottleneck, tollgate and parking-lot. We show that proposed framework outperform other baselines in three aspects: success rate, efficiency and safety.
